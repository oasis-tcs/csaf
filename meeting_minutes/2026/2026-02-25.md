- PR [#1165](https://github.com/oasis-tcs/csaf/pull/1165)
  - - Thomas Schmidt presents the pull request wrapping up a longer editing process to add an Extensions mechanism into CSAF 2.1.
    - Christoph Plutte suggests a value of 'important' rather than 'high_value'. Thomas Schmidt is not opposed to this wording.
    - Christoph Plutte brought up the concern that extensions potentially can be misused and could have negative, unintended side-effects and asked what mitigations should be implemented. He also discussed a specific case of another standard
    - Thomas Schmidt explained the TC has power over extensions and can deny-list an unfavorable extension if there is an extension the TC has a strong concern about.
    - Sonny van Lingen asked if this specific example is a concern for a CSAF extension too, to which Christoph Plutte clarified it was just an example to illustrate a possible risk of extensions in general.
    - Sonny van Lingen asked for more review time for this PR given the last edits have been completed less than 24 hours ago. He pointed out there has not been enough time to review.
    - Stefan Hagen pointed out it's possible to launch an email motion instead to not hamper progress. Sonny van Lingen does not oppose such an outcome.
    - A detailed discussion about Extensions followed. Thomas Schmidt clarified that ENISA, while trying to setup its SRP plans to rely on a CSAF extension for a specific profile.
    - Feng Cao pointed out that many users would like to piggyback on CSAF for certain use cases, but extensions should serve more than one user. Thomas discussed the two  important use-cases of extensions: allowing reporters to add data they feel is needed and to maintain interoperability i.e. new revisions of CVSS. Stefan Hagen explained that external dependencies there is no complete control - the TC can lobby and liaise.
    -  For PR #1165 An email motion will be circulated to the TC after the meeting, allowing a 7-day window for additional review. During this email motion, TC members and Editors collaborate to address comments. Any mitigatable comment will be included in this process. If there is no consensus, editors will help the officers to reject the motion.

  -    Stefan Hagen presents a motion to organize a full majority vote to adopt this standing rule. Sergii seconded. No discussions or objection.
